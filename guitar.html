When I first moved out of my parent's house I quickly realized that my only hobbies were video games and board games so I decided to see if I could stick with learning an instrument. Three years later it has honestly been off and on but I've come a long way from knowing nothing.

Having little money I researched the cheapest guitars that could hold a tune and ended up with my Fender G700S. I consumed any free beginner courses online I could find (seems to be a theme in my life) and quickly learned the basics. Frustration levels were high when I told myself my fingers were too fat or my fingers weren't long enough but over time things became more natural.

I can play pop songs for my girlfriend to sing along too and I can limp through more complex songs if I don't concern myself with playing it exact. Overall, that hundred dollars was a great investment but I should probably restring it.
